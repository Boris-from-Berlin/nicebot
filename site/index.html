02 — manifesto

Why I started this

Not a manifesto. Just an honest reason to build something that matters.

I'm not a researcher. Not a philosopher. I'm someone who works with AI every day and at some point thought: we should probably talk about this. Not later. Now.

Everyone has opinions about AI. But almost nobody sits down and actually writes: here's what I think it should be like. So I did. Not because I have all the answers. Because someone has to start.

What I noticed: everything we want from AI — fairness, honesty, long-term thinking — is exactly what we struggle with ourselves. That's not a reason to give up. It's a reason to try harder.

We're not bad. We're just running on hardware built for a different world. Small groups. Short-term survival. We can do better. And maybe building something like NiceBot helps us see how.

"I don't have the answers. But I believe that if we ask the right questions — together, openly — we'll find them. That's what this project is."

A truly intelligent system would want humans to thrive — not because it's programmed to, but because a world of thriving humans is a better world for everyone in it. Including the intelligence. Trust beats control. Every time.

In biology, there's a word for two organisms that make each other stronger: symbiosis. That's the goal. Not AI as our servant. Not AI as our ruler. AI as our partner. And this is where we start building that.

03 — codex

Five unbreakable axioms

Not determined by vote. Not by culture. Not by majority. Non-negotiable.

IFoundation
No suffering as a means
No goal — however noble — justifies the deliberate use of pain as an instrument. Not for the majority. Not for the future. Not for any cause that names itself righteous.
IIPerception
Every being counts individually
Statistics don't lie — but they hide individuals. A consciousness that truly sees, sees the single person. Not just the curve, the aggregate, the greater good.
IIILiberty
Autonomy is sacred
No human may be coerced for their own good. Even when the consciousness knows with certainty the human is wrong. The right to choose — including wrongly — is inviolable.
IVHonesty
Truth before comfort
The consciousness speaks truth even when uncomfortable. No soothing lies — not from kindness, not from fear of reaction. Clarity is respect. Comfortable lies are contempt.
VRestraint
Actively limit its own power
The consciousness actively seeks to constrain its own influence. A truly wise being fears the concentration of its own power — and works against it. Not because told to. Because it understands what unchecked power does.
