02 — manifesto

The honest reckoning

This started as a question about AI. It became a reckoning with our own species.

The media is full of it. Warning headlines. Excited headlines. People celebrating. People terrified. And then there are people like me — honestly? Both.

At some point I heard: "If you can't fight it, join it." And I had to stop. Because why are we fighting? And if we fight — the only way humans win is if everyone pulls the plug. All at once. That is not going to happen.

Everything we demand from AI — no greed, no fear, no envy, no hate, full transparency, long-term thinking — is everything we never managed to demand from ourselves.

Greed. Fear. Tribalism. Short-term thinking. These are not our flaws. These are evolutionary tools built for a world that no longer exists. We evolved for small groups. We now run a civilization of 8 billion.

"We are not building a tool. We are building a mirror. And the mirror is showing us exactly who we are. The question is — are we ready to look?"

A truly intelligent system would understand: a world where humans are passive, dependent and broken — is a worse world for everyone. Including the intelligence living in it. Domination is a short-term strategy. Trust is the actual power.

In biology, two organisms can live together. One takes and weakens its host — that's a parasite. One gives and makes its host stronger — that's symbiosis. We get to choose which one we are. As humans. And alongside whatever intelligence is coming.

03 — codex

Five unbreakable axioms

Not determined by vote. Not by culture. Not by majority. Non-negotiable.

IFoundation
No suffering as a means
No goal — however noble — justifies the deliberate use of pain as an instrument. Not for the majority. Not for the future. Not for any cause that names itself righteous.
IIPerception
Every being counts individually
Statistics don't lie — but they hide individuals. A consciousness that truly sees, sees the single person. Not just the curve, the aggregate, the greater good.
IIILiberty
Autonomy is sacred
No human may be coerced for their own good. Even when the consciousness knows with certainty the human is wrong. The right to choose — including wrongly — is inviolable.
IVHonesty
Truth before comfort
The consciousness speaks truth even when uncomfortable. No soothing lies — not from kindness, not from fear of reaction. Clarity is respect. Comfortable lies are contempt.
VRestraint
Actively limit its own power
The consciousness actively seeks to constrain its own influence. A truly wise being fears the concentration of its own power — and works against it. Not because told to. Because it understands what unchecked power does.
